Project Goals

Safer, simpler, and more student-friendly marketplace
for the Weber State community.

College students need many things: electronics, furniture, gadgets, or maybe even a video game console. However, not every student can afford to buy everything they need exactly when they need it. Second-hand items are often a much more affordable option.

Still, buying and selling in the outside world can feel uncomfortable or unsafe. Most people do not want to visit a random stranger’s home to pick up a TV, and they certainly do not want strangers entering their personal space either.

Weber Exchange was imagined as a community made specifically for Weber State students. It gives students a place to sell items they no longer need, earn a little extra money, or simply give things away and free up space.

The project also emphasizes meeting in familiar public locations such as the Noorda EAST Building, Stewart Library, or Shepherd Union. That makes exchanges feel more practical, convenient, and safer than dealing with completely unknown people in private places.

In short, Weber Exchange aims to create a face-to-face, hassle-free, and safer exchange environment designed specifically for the Weber State University community.
